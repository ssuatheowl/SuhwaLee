Payment Alert

01Project Summary

UX design

User research

Usability testing

Copy writing

Do you have the experience of checking your banking app numerous times per day on a specific day of every month, only to see that your salary has not reached your account yet?
At ABN Amro, we have turned the wasted effort into positive anticipation by implementing a payment alert service on the mobile banking app. We designed a flexible alert service that could be tailored to the individual user needs. The users could customize the settings to get informed of any transaction events of interest in their payment accounts.

02UX & Flow design

The flow to access and use the feature was designed in wireframes. Flows to promote the new feature to the existing users and new users was designed additionally.

More flowcharts were made to communicate complex interaction between front-end and different back-end services, which helped internal alignment between various teams.

03UX / UI design

Through guerrilla testing the design was fine-tuned to improve usability.

The copy for the push notification was decided based on the input from 100+ users.

We decided to include last 4 digits of an account along with the transactional amount in the notification. This was decided based the common user behavior that the majority of the users did not give any custom name to the account, which meant that the account name would have been the user's name.
So the only differentiator between accounts would only be the account numbers. Since the users felt the account number was a sensitive information to reveal via push notification, we decided to encrypt except for the last 4 digit to protect user data.

04Results

In December 2019, the Payment alert (betaalalert) was released on ABN Amro's mobile banking app for payment accounts. The opt-in rate for the service sharply increased which also had a positive impact on the NPS score. We have noticed that many colleagues had set up an alert for the incoming salary. It was fun to hear the synchronous sounds coming from the payment alert every payday at the office!

SMARTPHONE CONTROLLED HOME ENVIRONMENTS


Can Gesture control be smart?

The concept introduces a smart, effortless, and natural gesture library for controlling smartphone and Smarthome environments, which utilizes natural actions/behaviors as a trigger.


Master Thesis Project / TU Delft / 2015-2016

  • # INTERACTION DESIGN
  • # CONCEPT DESIGN
  • # DIGITAL DESIGN
  • # APPLICATION

CONCEPT

SUMMARY

The interaction concept for a smartphone control includes twelve smartphone built-in functions triggered by gestures. Most of the gestures are designed to be a part of actions/behaviors that we are already doing, thus it gives a feeling of automation and smartness.

The twelve functions are:
‘Wake up the device’, ‘Make a call’, ‘Answer a call’, ‘Snooze a call’, ’Reject a call’, ‘Launch the messenger’, ‘Active the camera’, ‘Snooze the alarm’, ‘Turn off the alarm’, ‘Launch the recorder’, ‘Launch the music app’, and ‘Start remote control’. The lastly mentioned function extends the usage of a smartphone in home environments, while the rest works as a shortcut for the control of a smartphone itself.



BENEFIT

The concept contributes to an intuitive use and control of smartphone and Smarthome, by saving interaction time, physical and mental efforts and social awkwardness to control them.
Most of the features work as a shortcut by removing steps in the original smartphone interaction, thus it saves time and physical effort.
Another benefit is a reduced mental effort by reducing distractions. The gesture control can be done without looking at the screen. This reduces the chance of being lost in the screen with tons of icons. Furthermore, as users can feel and see their own gestures, they are constantly reminded of the intended task to be done.
Social awkwardness is improved, by allowing the user to focus less on the smartphone screen.

DESIGN PROCESS







USER RESEARCHES

#1   PRELIMINARY GESTURE LIBRARY

The preliminarily selected gestures were evaluated through a usertest for the assessment. The main objective of this research was to verify if each of the gestures had the potential to be implemented as a new gesture concept for smartphone. Participants received an online survey, followed by an individual interview session. In the online survey, the participants were given a description explaining the basic idea of the concept. Thereafter, they were asked to evaluate gesture and function pairs, concerning a given task. Each gestures were presented with pictures, or videos if necessary. The evaluation criteria for the assessment were intuitiveness, ease of use, and social awkwardness, which were to be rated on a scale from 1 (very poor)-5 (very good).



#2   CONCEPT SELECTION

This user test aimed to see the user preference on the previous solutions proposed for the concept. It covered the assessment of gesture library, and the application trigger scenario. The contents were built in Wireframe, to evoke discussion mainly on the interactions and functionality of the concepts, instead of focusing on minor application design elements. The concepts were delivered to the participants in a form of a paper prototype. The participants were given with a real smartphone along with the paper prototype. The participants could try out the gestures while interacting the real smartphone.



#3   EVALUATION

Using a partially working prototype and Wizard-of-Oz method, the final design was tested with potential users. The visual and haptic feedback was prototyped to give an experience close to real-life, while gesture recognition itself was simulated by the wizard. The wizard processed a user's gesture and gave a command to the system, to give the output that corresponds to the user's gestural input.
